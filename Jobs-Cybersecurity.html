Cybersecurity

Information Security Analyst

  • Entry-Level Average Salary: $70,000/year
  • Mid-Level Average Salary: $85,000/year
  • Senior-Level Average Salary: $105,000/year
  • Job Description: Works on the maintenance, monitoring and support of information security initiatives, including password management. Additionally, you will monitor and protect networks from cyber threats and install security tools.
  • Lifestyle: As an Information Security Analyst, you will have the ability to work remotely, though some on-call work may be required during security breaches or the installation of security tools. Work hours are not as flexible as other jobs, with a typical Monday-Friday schedule. However, working on weekends, holidays, and non-business hours may be required.

Penetration Tester (Ethical Hacker)

  • Entry-Level Average Salary: $85,000/year
  • Mid-Level Average Salary: $105,000/year
  • Senior-Level Average Salary: $125,000/year
  • Job Description: You will conduct manual penetration testing on web applications, APIs, cloud environments, infrastructure, mobile applications, and custom technologies. You will also provide security advice and collaborate with engineering teams and non-technical partners. Additionally, you'll develop scripts and tools to augment penetration testing activities. In summary, you will simulate cyberattacks to identify system security weaknesses and report them.
  • Lifestyle: As a Penetration Tester, you will typically work remotely, although some companies may require on-site work. Hours are generally flexible, though you will still be expected to work 40 hours per week. The work is project-based and may involve tight deadlines and collaboration with other workers.

Security Consultant

  • Entry-Level Average Salary: $95,000/year
  • Mid-Level Average Salary: $120,000/year
  • Senior-Level Average Salary: $140,000/year
  • Job Description: You will support teams working to evaluate and secure infrastructures. As a Security Consultant, you will assess and improve organizations' security systems, providing expert advice.
  • Lifestyle: As a Security Consultant, you will typically work with multiple clients, and frequent travel may be required. The work hours are flexible, and most of the work is remote.
